001 — Introduction

Not a restaurant.
A moment.

Origins exists at the intersection of gastronomy, material culture, and performance. It is not a service. It is an event of the senses — singular, unrepeatable, and designed to dissolve the boundary between eating and art.

"Each experience is conceived as a living installation — where what you taste, see, smell, and touch forms a single, coherent language."

Origins is conceived for those who believe that luxury is not the abundance of things, but the precision of an experience. Every element — the table, the light, the first gesture of a hand — is choreographed with the same intentionality as a work of art.



It does not replicate. It does not repeat. Each Origins experience is created for a specific moment, a specific place, and a specific audience — then released into memory.

002 — Vision & Positioning

Where gastronomy
becomes territory.

Origins does not position itself in the restaurant industry, the catering market, or the events sector. It occupies an entirely different space — one defined not by category, but by intention.

I

Rare by Design

Origins never produces the same experience twice. Scarcity is not a strategy — it is a principle. Each commission is a unique act of creation, limited to a single occasion.

II

Art as Method

The approach is that of an artist, not a caterer. Food, space, light, and gesture are all treated as compositional elements in an immersive installation.

III

Silence as Luxury

Origins communicates with restraint. No noise, no spectacle for its own sake. The experience speaks through what is present — and what is deliberately left absent.

IV

The Private Sphere

Origins operates exclusively in the private sector. No public bookings, no open tables. Every experience begins with a conversation, and ends with an invitation.

V

International Ambition

Conceived in Europe, Origins is designed to travel. The concept adapts to any space — a Moroccan riad, a Parisian hôtel particulier, a Venetian palazzo.

VI

A Living Legacy

Every Origins experience is documented as a work — photographed, archived, never replicated. The guest leaves not with a memory of a dinner, but a memory of an artwork.

003 — Artistic Direction

The visual vocabulary
of Origins

Visual Universe

Origins draws from the aesthetic vocabulary of still life painting, Japanese wabi-sabi, and contemporary art installation. The visual world is defined by raw textures against perfect white surfaces, extreme macro photography of natural matter, and the contrast between the organic and the geometric. Color is used sparingly — the palette belongs to nature: oxidized iron, chalk, wet stone, bone, charred wood, deep wine.

Lighting & Atmosphere

All light is directional and considered. Hard single-source light that carves shadow and texture. No ambient fill. Candles used architecturally — as vertical elements, as a field of warmth in darkness. The visual atmosphere references Caravaggio's chiaroscuro transposed into contemporary space.

Cinematic Language

Slow. Deliberate. No handheld urgency. A cinema of contemplation — close-ups of hands, of surfaces, of steam rising in silence. The camera moves like memory: slowly, without cuts, letting the texture of time enter the frame.

Materials & Elements

  • Raw stone and mineral surfaces
  • Fire — flame, smoke, ember, ash
  • Unglazed ceramic, oxidized metal
  • Human hands — the gesture of making
  • Living botanical elements
  • Water — still, condensation, ice
  • Aged wood and rough linen
  • Colored glass and natural resin
  • Wax — candle, seal, patina
  • Extreme close-up of organic matter

004 — The Experience

Before, during,
after.

The Origins experience is not measured in courses. It is measured in states of consciousness.

Before — The Invitation

Anticipation as
first act

The guest receives not a confirmation, but an object. A handwritten card. A sealed envelope. A scent. The experience has already begun — in the space between invitation and arrival. Anticipation is designed, not accidental.

1
During — The Immersion

Time moves
differently

Upon arrival, the guest enters a space that has been entirely conceived for them — and only for them. The table is a landscape. The food arrives not as courses but as gestures. Every element is a proposition, never an obligation. The guest is simultaneously audience and protagonist.

2
After — The Residue

What remains
in the body

Origins does not end when the last plate is removed. The experience continues in the guest long after — in the memory of a texture, a smell encountered at the table, a conversation born in that particular light. The mark is not visible. It is felt.

005 — Instagram Strategy

The feed as
silent film.

Origins does not perform on social media. It curates a window — refined, rare, and unmistakably itself. The feed is an artwork in its own right.

Feed Architecture

A strict 3-column rhythm alternates between three visual registers: the extreme close-up (texture, matter, detail), the wide environmental shot (table, space, atmosphere), and the process — hands working, steam rising, a gesture mid-way. No caption is more than two sentences. No hashtag visible in the grid.

Tone & Voice

Spare. Poetic. Never explanatory. The caption completes the image — it does not describe it. Origins writes in the present tense, from the interior. One sentence. Sometimes a fragment. Often a silence.

Reels

Shot in slow motion or real time with no acceleration. Ambient sound only — never music unless the experience itself contained music. Duration: 15 to 40 seconds. One idea per reel. The camera never rushes.

007 — Website Structure

A digital space
as still as stone.

The Origins website does not sell. It reveals — slowly, deliberately, as the experience itself does.

Page 01

Home

Full-screen opening — a single visual, no text for the first three seconds. Then: ORIGINS appears in fade. One sentence below. A slow scroll invitation. No menu visible until the cursor moves.

"There are meals you eat.
And then there are Origins."
Page 02

Concept

Long-form text, wide margins, generous white space. No images — only typography and a single fine-line illustration. The page reads like a manifesto, not a service description.

"Origins is not a catering company. It is a proposition. An invitation to reconsider what a table can mean."
Page 03

Experience

Three chapters — Before / During / After — presented as a vertical scroll with slow image transitions. Each chapter has one text block and one full-bleed visual. Navigation between chapters is gestural.

"The experience begins before you arrive. It ends long after you leave."
Page 04

Gallery

A curated archive of past experiences — presented as a grid of large, uncaptioned images. No dates, no client names. Click to enter full-screen mode. The scroll is horizontal, not vertical.

"Each experience is documented once, archived, and never repeated."
Page 05

Private Bookings

Not a form — a letter. "To commission an Origins experience is to begin a conversation." A single email address. A short paragraph describing the process: inquiry, dialogue, creation, experience. No pricing. No packages.

"Every experience begins with a conversation. Write to us. We will respond."
Page 06

Contact

Minimal. A single email. An optional city (Paris / available internationally). No phone number. No social links in the main body — only in the footer. The page communicates through restraint.

"origins.experience@—
Paris, and wherever the invitation leads."
008 — Photo & Video Direction

How to photograph
what cannot be held.

Scenes to Capture

  • 01 Hands arranging a single element on a raw surface
  • 02 A candle flame in an otherwise dark space
  • 03 Steam rising from a bowl — extreme close up, macro
  • 04 The table seen from above before the guests arrive
  • 05 A cross-section of an ingredient — okra, citrus, fennel
  • 06 Flowers used architecturally — single stem, radical composition
  • 07 The pour — liquid into glass, shot from the side in hard light
  • 08 A guest's profile in low light — never their face directly
  • 09 The empty table after — residue, trace, memory
  • 10 Abstract: condensation on cold stone, ash, smoke dissolving

Camera & Technical Direction

  • Lens Prime lenses only — 50mm & 100mm macro for stills
  • Motion Slow dolly or static tripod — no handheld
  • Frame Off-center composition — subject never centered
  • DoF Shallow — one element in focus, all else dissolved
  • FPS 60fps slowed to 24fps in post — organic motion
  • Color Desaturated warm base — grain in shadows

Lighting Setup

  • Key Single hard source — window, spot, or naked bulb
  • Fill None, or minimal reflector — deep shadows preserved
  • Mood Chiaroscuro — 60% of frame in shadow minimum
  • Color 3200K tungsten warmth, or cool daylight at golden hour
  • Night Candle light supplemented by a single 150W practical
